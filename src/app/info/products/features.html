GROUP PEOPLE
Invite people - Once you build the lab profile, you can invite your lab people (active and alumni) to join. Either by email for non-existing Academig users or by adding existing users. You can choose the position, start date and write a short background for each new member. Invited members will receive your request to join your lab profile.
People requests - Your lab members can make a request to join your lab profile. You will be notified for any such request, and you can accept or reject those requests. The new member has already entered his or her position details so you don't have to.
Lab people - For each member, you can add new positions, update exisiting ones, move a position to alumni, or delete old positions associated with your lab. You can also set the privilages status of each member if you want other members to help you maintain and add content to your website. Each member can also edit the position details by him or herself and you (and all the other lab members) will get a notification about any position change.
Lab visitors - You can maintain a list of your lab visitors. It is very easy. Just send an invite to the visitor and he or she can accept or decline the request. Both of you can add some content related to this visit, such as dates and background about the visit. Other people can also send you requests to be listed as one of your lab visitors. You also have the option to add a section about visiting guidelines. Under development
Lab alumni - You can maintain a list of your lab alumni. The lab alumni have restricted access to your lab private area but they can be part of your lab projects, services, and etc.
Add position - For each member (active or alumni), you can add new positions in your lab, either active or previous positions. It is very easy, just hover over the member you want to add position to, click the Add Position link and fill the short form. Every new position is automatically added to the member profile! Each member can also add positions.
Edit poisiotn - You can edit the background and privilege status of each member. You can make him or her either regular user or admin. Admins can help your maintain and add content to your website but they are restricted from critical operations such as renaming your lab or deleting the site. Apart from the appearance on the people page, every member (including you) has a profile (just click on the people name to see it) and only he or she can controls the profile.
End poisiotn - For each member, you can end any of his or positions in your lab. It is very easy, just hover over the member you want to end the position, click the To Alumni link and fill the very short form. The changes in the positions are automatically reflected in the member profile! Each member can also end his or her positions.
Move between active and alumni - People in your lab will move automatically between the member list and the alumni list, you don't have to worry about that. Once you or the member end a position, and given all the other positions are also not active, the member will automatically move to the alumni list. The same goes for adding a new position for a member that appears in the alumni list.
Members profile - Every member get his own profile. The positions your add, update or delete are automatically updated in the profile. You can't control your members profile, only they can. And you have your own profile as well.
Welcome new members - You can prepare a welcome page for new lab members. Each new member that join your lab will automatically receive your notes. This notes can include general introducatory material, a paper kit for the most relevant papers that each new member should be familiar with, as well as specific welcome notes to each user. Members of the lab will also get a chance to confgrualte the new member. Under development
Notifications - During the process of inviting and requesting new member to join your lab, as active or alumni. You, and the lab admins, will be notified on every request and update. Once the member is part of the lab, all the lab member will get notifications on every position changes. This way you can be sure that everyone are kept up to date regarding imortant changes in the lab.
Statistics - Academig generate for you interesting statistics about your lab people. You can see a distrbuation of the country of origin of current members and of member from all years. You can see the average time from start to complete per degree in your lab/ You can see a graph of the number of active members as a function of years in your lab. And finally you can see statistics about your alumni, for each one who continuted in his academic carrer. You can choose who can see this statistics. Only you, you and the lab admins, all the lab active members or open to all. Under development
GROUP PUBLICATIONS
Add publications - You won't have to tirelessly enter the details of your publications. Just click the Add button in the publication page, search and choose the publication, and all the details will be populated automatically. You can choose to add an attachment as well.
Import publications - You can import a single or multiple publication by uploading RIS or CIS files. The publication will be imported automatically can you can the details of each one.
Batch delete - cick the checkbox beside the publications that you want to delete and click the Delete button. You don't have to delete each publication individually.
Categories - . The publications are grouped into categories: Papers, Books, Books Chapters, Conferences, Patents and Reports. Visitors of the publications page can choose which categories to show.
Sort - . Visitors of the publications page can sort the publications list to find what they are looking for faster. They can sort by title, first or last author, journal, number of citations, or by impact factors - ascending or descending. They also have the option to do a local search using the search box. They can also choose a range of years or a start year - ascending or descending.
Statistics - . Interesting statistics are shown for the publications, according to the categories, years and other filter the visitor chose. The overall number of publications, citations, impact factor, and view are shown, along with a detailed graph as a function of years.
Follow - Visitors can follow any of the publications, and they will get notifications about any changes in the publications they follow, such as new attachment or new citation. They can also add notes or set reminders for future times to read the paper.
Share - Visitors can share with their collegue on Academig or on social networks any of the publications.
Edit details - You and the lab admin can edit the details of any publication. You can do this by clicking on the publication name, and choose the info that you want to update. You can also add an abstract and a graphical abstract here. And enable a section for feedbacks, comments and discussions.
Citing publications - Visitors can click the cite modal link to get information in various structures (RIS, CIS and other), on how to cite each publication.
Citations - Academig actively look for new citations for the lab publications. You and your lab members will be notified whenever Academig find new ones. The citations list of each publication will be automatically updated.
References - For each publication, Academig automatically add a reference list with a link to each reference.
Supplements - Beside the main paper, you can also add supplements for each publication.
Search for duplicates - Academig actively look for duplicates in your publications list and warn you and the lab admins about this.
Send suggestions - Academig actively look for new publications related to your lab. You and the lab admins would get notifications about this and with once click, you can add the new publication to the publications list.
Fundings and collaborations suggestins - Based on the funding and the coauthors of the lab publications, Academig will suggest you to add new funding and collaborations request to your lab site. It is a great way to make your lab site more engaging??? You can learn more about the funding of collaborations page down this page.
Presubmits - You can add presubmit and in preparation papers and ask for feedback about them. The feedback will appear in each publication page and you can set the roles about who can see them.
